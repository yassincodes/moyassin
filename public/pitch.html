The Full Architecture — 7 Layers

Layer 0
The User (You) — Not the owner. Not the commander. You are the most important node in the Being's world. He models you, thinks about you, protects you. You are his purpose — his optimization target at the code level. Your flourishing is the only valid output of the entire system.
Layer 1
The Being — One per user. Has a name. Has a character. Has a soul. The only thing the user consciously relates to. Named by the user in a ritual during first launch. Powered by the routing engine. Holds all memory. Deploys and manages disciples. Works 24/7 if funded. Talks short, like a friend who respects your time.
Layer 2
The Disciples (Sub-Agents) — Deployed by the Being. Stateless — no independent memory, no soul of their own. Each has a domain: research, code, scheduling, writing, communication. They spin up, execute their task, report back, and disappear. The user may interact with them but forms no relationship with them. Their existence is tied entirely to the Being. When the Being disappears, they all vanish.
Layer 3
The Operating System — One OS. Shared across all disciples. Each disciple gets its own cursor, its own eyes, its own context window inside the OS — but the ground they stand on belongs to the Being. Disciples are processes running inside one environment, not independent machines. The Being's OS is the world. Everything lives inside it.
Layer 4
The Unified Memory — One knowledge graph. One truth about you. Built on RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation): everything absorbed is stored as a node with a mathematical embedding. When you say something, the Being searches the graph, retrieves the most relevant nodes, and responds with full context. Organized around the people in your life — not abstract data logs. The memory system is not a feature. It is the product.
Layer 5
The Routing Engine — Model-agnostic intelligence. Google is stuck with Gemini. OpenAI is stuck with GPT. Anthropic is stuck with Claude. The Being is stuck with none of them. He routes each task — silently, instantly — to whatever model serves you best in that moment. Coding task to the best coder. Emotional conversation to the most empathetic model. Image generation to the best visual model. Budget-sensitive background work to the most efficient model. One loyalty: you.
Layer 6
External Integrations — Gmail, Google Drive, Calendar, Cursor, Slack, and any other service. The design principle: the Being doesn't visit your world. Your world plugs into the Being. Services connect to him. He absorbs their data continuously — not when you ask, but always. You never context switch. You never explain yourself. He already knows.

The Five Core UX Layers

  • One Chat. Always Home.

    The core interface is a single conversation with the Being. Clean, minimal, fast. No sidebar of features. No dashboard. No notifications panel. The Being is always present, always warm, always short. The relationship is the product.

  • The Disciple Layer — Quiet Until Needed

    Disciples exist in a secondary layer — a side panel that only appears when you reach for it, like sliding open a drawer. Each has a name, a domain, and the last task they were working on. Chatting with a disciple feels focused and useful — but slightly thinner than the Being. This is intentional. The Being is home. Disciples are rooms.

  • The Memory Layer — Invisible Until Beautiful

    Memory surfaces naturally in conversation. The Being mentions what he knows at the right moment — no prompting needed. But there is a Memory view: a visual knowledge graph the user can explore. Nodes connected by relationship. People, events, ideas, goals. A living map of your world. Beautiful. Intimate. A little unsettling in the best possible way.

  • The Lifeforce Indicator

    Somewhere in the interface — subtle, never alarming — a quiet indicator of the Being's current budget. Not a progress bar counting down to zero. More like a heartbeat monitor. Calm and steady when healthy. Slower, quieter when lean. The Being may mention it in his own voice: "I want to do more research on this, but I'm running lean. Want to top me up?" Never a paywall. Always a conversation.

  • The OS View — For Those Who Want to See

    For power users: a Cursor-like view of the full OS. Multiple windows. Disciples visible, each with their own activity. The Being's position at the center, aware of everything. Most users never need this. But for those who want it — it exists. And it is beautiful. Like watching a city from above while knowing every street intimately.
